Pharmacy Scheduler

Pharmacy Scheduler is an application built around scheduling COVID-19 testing and vaccination appointments for Auburn University and eventually the greater Auburn-Opelika area. The purpose of this application is to allow medical administration to create testing and vaccination events to streamline the relief process. Users are invited to schedule an appointment on a certain day to be tested or vaccinated. This application includes an automatic email system based around the appointments. At the time of this writing, it is currently not public facing and only available to Auburn University students/faculty. Here are a few pictures of site from an average user's perspective. Auburn Answers

The Auburn Answers project is an Auburn Student Government Association (SGA) initiative to increase student involvement with campus-wide decisions. Students, faculty, and others can fill out polls, submit questions and comments, and get in touch with SGA representatives. This project has a heavy back-end focus, particularly with allowing SGA administrators to interact with student's submissions and set up polls. Though this is primarily meant for Auburn students, it is available to the general public to interact with and submit responses related to Auburn's campus. Please click the link above and explore!

SGA Voting

SGA elections at any university are always a very busy time. People everywhere campaigning for their candidate, plenty of platforms to view and speeches to attend, not to mention the craziness of voting day. This project was meant to consolidate all of those things into one central location. The SGA Voting application allows students to vote for their candidate of choice day-of using an easy, mobile-friendly online interface. Admins are able to setup elections and configure them to their needs as well as view demographics and reports following a big election. Students are able to vote with 3 simple clicks! A recent inclusion in this project was the ability to create Ranked Choice Elections, where voters can rank their top x choices, rather than be forced to only choose one candidate. Flunch

Flunch is a portmanteau of the words "Faculty" and "Lunch". As the name may suggest, this application allows students and faculty to schedule free lunches anywhere on campus with one another. The goal is to allow students to garner better relationships with their professors and other faculty members. These "flunches" are meant primarily for students to discuss grades, research, and other opportunities. Students may request up to three potential dates and times for a flunch, and faculty members may accept the invitation for one of the times or they may suggest a new time for them to meet. What better way to discuss your failing grade than over a free lunch on campus? This site is only for Auburn University members, so I've included some images below of the student's requesting process.
